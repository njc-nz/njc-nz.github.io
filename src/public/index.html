What is Judo

Judo is a Japanese martial art created by Jigoro Kano in 1882 as a system of moral and physical development. It was introduced to the Olympic Games in 1964 and has grown in popularity world wide. It is now a highly competitive international sport.

Judo means “the gentle way” and focuses on using an opponent’s own body weight and momentum to unbalance and throw them. The objective is to throw an opponent on their back and then either pin them on the ground or get them to submit with an arm-lock or choke technique (only those aged 14+ learn arm-locks and chokes in a controlled environment). Judo does not include strikes, kicks, or the use of weapons.

Benefits of Judo

Build self-confidence and mental resilience

• Improve your flexibility, strength, and endurance while having fun
• Learn self-defence
• Make new friends and become part of a supportive community
• Optional opportunities to engage in competitions
• Worldwide recognised grading system

Judo is excellent for kids as it is safe (doesn’t involve strikes or kicks and everyone learns how to fall from throws properly) and promotes physical, mental, and social skill development. International studies have even shown that judo is the safest sport for under 13 year olds.

Classes

New to Judo? Give it a go!

We welcome everyone to come try judo! Whether you’ve just heard about judo or you’ve dabbled in some similar sports or martial arts, you’re welcome to come along to a class to see if you like it.

We offer a couple of trial classes so that you can decide if you want to continue. Everyone who gets on the mat must have their details registered with our club in case of an emergency. • Beginners - open to beginners of all ages. A great place to start judo, learn some fundamentals before progressing into a juniors or seniors class.
• Pewees - under 7 year olds.
• Juniors - mainly 7- 13 year olds. All experience levels are welcome.
• Seniors - teenagers and adults. All experience levels are welcome.
• Women's class - a class for women, run by women, all grades welcome.
• Jurrasic-judo - fundamentals/technique-focused class for all grades and ages (mostly attended by adults)

Fees

Family Discount

We offer a family discount on our term fees. When there are two or more members from the same family, a $10 per member discount will be applied to the family’s total term fees. E.g. Term fees for a family with 2 children attending classes: (2x 85) - (2x 10) = $150 per term, which is a $20 discount.

Beginner, Peewee, and Junior classes are run during school terms and not on public holidays. Senior and Jurassic Judo classes generally continue through school holidays (between terms) and on public holidays.

Van tournaments

These are friendly competitions between the clubs of the Central Districts Area - Nelson Judo Club, Motueka Judo Club, Blenheim Judo Club, and Granity Judo Club. The clubs take turns hosting. While primarily for the kids, there are often opportunities for adults to compete too. Van tournaments are great opportunities to get to know the other judoka and their families in our region.

Competitions

Judo New Zealand holds several competitions every year. These draw judoka from across the country as well as from overseas. Divisions are based on gender, age, and weight. See the Judo New Zealand Events Calendar for up to date information: Judo New Zealand
